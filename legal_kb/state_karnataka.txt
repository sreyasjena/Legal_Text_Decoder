KARNATAKA STATE SPECIFIC LAWS KNOWLEDGE BASE
=============================================
Comprehensive reference for Karnataka-specific laws, regulations, and legal procedures.

─────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 1: KARNATAKA PROPERTY AND LAND LAWS
─────────────────────────────────────────

1. KARNATAKA LAND REVENUE ACT, 1964
Governs land administration, revenue records, and mutations in Karnataka.
(a) RTC (Record of Rights, Tenancy and Crops) — also called Pahani — is the primary land record document showing ownership, tenancy, and crop details.
(b) Mutation (Khata transfer): Change in ownership records in revenue records after sale, inheritance, or gift. Done at the Tahsildar's office.
(c) Phodi: Partition of joint land holdings recorded in revenue records.
(d) Survey numbers: Each plot of land has a unique survey number in Karnataka.
(e) Bhoomi portal: Karnataka's online land records portal (bhoomi.karnataka.gov.in) — citizens can access RTCs, mutation status, and land records online.

2. KARNATAKA STAMP ACT, 1957
Governs stamp duty on instruments in Karnataka.
(a) Sale deed: 5% stamp duty + 1% registration fee + surcharges (BBMP cess, etc.). Total approximately 6.8% to 7.5%.
(b) Gift deed: Same as sale deed between non-relatives. Reduced rates for blood relatives (spouse, children, grandchildren, parents — typically 1% to 2%).
(c) Partition deed: 2% on market value of separated share.
(d) Mortgage deed: 0.5% on loan amount (maximum Rs. 10 lakhs stamp duty).
(e) Leave and license agreement: Rs. 200 to Rs. 500 under Article 30(1)(i) for agreements up to 11 months.
(f) Power of attorney: Rs. 1,000 to Rs. 2,000 depending on type.
(g) e-STAMPS: Available through SHCIL and Karnataka Bank. Certificate number starts with IN-KA.

3. KAVERI ONLINE SERVICES — KARNATAKA REGISTRATION
Karnataka's property registration portal (kaverionline.karnataka.gov.in):
(a) Online booking of appointment for property registration.
(b) Online calculation of stamp duty and registration fees.
(c) Upload of documents for pre-scrutiny.
(d) Property registration at Sub-Registrar's office.
(e) Encumbrance Certificate (EC) search and download — shows all registered transactions.
(f) Registered document status and verification.

4. KARNATAKA APARTMENT OWNERSHIP ACT, 1972
Governs apartment ownership in Karnataka:
(a) Apartment can be individually owned with undivided share in common areas.
(b) Apartment Owners Association: Mandatory for maintenance and management.
(c) Declaration of Apartment: Filed with Sub-Registrar — contains floor plan, share of common areas.
(d) Resale: Apartment can be sold, mortgaged, bequeathed independently of other apartments.

5. KARNATAKA AGRICULTURAL LAND (RESTRICTIONS ON TRANSFER AND PROVISIONS FOR ALLOTMENT) ACT
(a) Agricultural land in Karnataka cannot be purchased by non-agriculturists (Section 79A and 79B of Karnataka Land Reforms Act, 1961).
(b) Conversion required: Agricultural land must be converted to non-agricultural use before constructing buildings or using for non-agricultural purposes.
(c) Conversion application: Filed with the Deputy Commissioner's office.
(d) BDA (Bangalore Development Authority) and BBMP: Have jurisdiction over land use in their respective areas.

SECTION 2: KARNATAKA RENT AND HOUSING LAWS
─────────────────────────────────────────

6. KARNATAKA RENT ACT, 1999
(a) Applies to buildings in urban local body areas in Karnataka.
(b) Standard rent: Rent fixed by Rent Authority — landlord cannot charge more.
(c) Permitted increases: 10% every 3 years.
(d) Eviction grounds: Non-payment of rent, subletting, nuisance, bona fide personal requirement, reconstruction.
(e) Rent Authority: Civil Judge (Junior Division) or JMFC — jurisdiction over rent disputes.
(f) Leave and license agreements properly executed: Generally outside Rent Act protection.

7. BBMP (BRUHAT BENGALURU MAHANAGARA PALIKE) REGULATIONS
(a) Property tax: Calculated on Self-Assessment System (SAS) — based on built-up area, zone, property usage. Paid annually.
(b) Khata: Municipal record of property ownership in BBMP area. A-Khata for regularized properties, B-Khata for irregular/revenue site properties.
(c) Building plan approval: Required for any new construction or addition. Filed with BBMP or BDA.
(d) Occupancy Certificate: Issued by BBMP/BDA after construction — required for legal occupation.
(e) BBMP solid waste management: Registration for commercial establishments.

8. KARNATAKA RERA (K-RERA)
Karnataka Real Estate Regulatory Authority:
(a) Website: rera.karnataka.gov.in
(b) All residential and commercial real estate projects with area >500 sq. m. or 8 units must be registered.
(c) Complaint filing: Homebuyers can file complaints online.
(d) Adjudicating Officer: For compensation claims.
(e) Appellate Tribunal: For appeals against RERA authority orders.

SECTION 3: KARNATAKA EMPLOYMENT AND LABOUR LAWS
─────────────────────────────────────────

9. KARNATAKA SHOPS AND COMMERCIAL ESTABLISHMENTS ACT, 1961
(a) Applies to shops, commercial establishments, restaurants, theatres, and other establishments in Karnataka.
(b) Registration: Mandatory within 30 days of starting business.
(c) Working hours: Maximum 9 hours per day, 48 hours per week.
(d) Weekly holiday: One day off per week.
(e) Overtime: Double wages for overtime work.
(f) Leave: 12 days casual leave, 12 days sick leave per year. Earned leave accumulates at 1 day per 20 days worked.
(g) Notice period: 1 month notice for employees with 6+ months of service.
(h) Women employees: Cannot be employed after 8 PM without consent and adequate safety measures.
(i) Termination: Written notice required. No termination during leave period.

10. KARNATAKA INDUSTRIAL ESTABLISHMENTS (NATIONAL AND FESTIVAL HOLIDAYS) ACT, 1963
(a) Provides for national and festival holidays for employees in Karnataka.
(b) National holidays: Republic Day (Jan 26), Independence Day (Aug 15), Gandhi Jayanti (Oct 2).
(c) Festival holidays: Ugadi, Kannad Rajyotsava, Deepavali, Christmas, Eid, and others — 8 festival holidays per year.

11. KARNATAKA FACTORIES ACT COMPLIANCE
Karnataka follows the Factories Act, 1948 for manufacturing establishments:
(a) Registration of factory with Director of Factories.
(b) Health and safety measures for workers.
(c) Working hours, overtime, and leave provisions.
(d) Annual leave with wages: 1 day for every 20 days worked.

SECTION 4: KARNATAKA TAX LAWS
─────────────────────────────────────────

12. PROFESSIONAL TAX IN KARNATAKA
(a) Levied under Karnataka Tax on Professions, Trades, Callings and Employment Act, 1976.
(b) Employees earning Rs. 15,000 or more per month pay Rs. 200 per month (Rs. 2,400 per year).
(c) Employers must deduct professional tax from employee salaries and deposit with state government.
(d) Self-employed professionals: Pay professional tax based on income slab.
(e) Exemptions: Persons above 60 years, persons with disabilities, parents of children with disabilities.

13. KARNATAKA VALUE ADDED TAX AND GST
(a) GST (Goods and Services Tax): Implemented from July 2017, replaced Karnataka VAT, service tax, and other indirect taxes.
(b) GST rates: 0%, 5%, 12%, 18%, 28% depending on goods/services category.
(c) GST registration: Mandatory for businesses with turnover exceeding Rs. 40 lakhs (Rs. 20 lakhs for service providers, Rs. 10 lakhs for special category states).
(d) GSTIN: Goods and Services Tax Identification Number — Karnataka GST code starts with "29".

─────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 5: KARNATAKA BUSINESS REGULATIONS
─────────────────────────────────────────

14. KARNATAKA INDUSTRIES (FACILITATION) ACT, 2002
(a) Single-window clearance for industrial projects in Karnataka.
(b) Udyog Mitra: Online portal for investment facilitation (karnatakaudyogmitra.gov.in).
(c) Deemed approval: If government does not respond within specified time, approval is deemed granted.
(d) Karnataka Industrial Areas Development Board (KIADB): Develops industrial areas and acquires land for industries.
(e) Karnataka Industrial Policy 2020-2025: Incentives for investment in various sectors.

15. BBMP TRADE LICENSE
(a) Required for all commercial establishments in BBMP area.
(b) Annual renewal required.
(c) Required for: shops, restaurants, hotels, clinics, schools, industries.
(d) Application through BBMP's online portal.

16. FSSAI LICENSE IN KARNATAKA
(a) Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI) license required for all food businesses.
(b) Basic registration: Annual turnover below Rs. 12 lakhs.
(c) State license: Annual turnover Rs. 12 lakhs to Rs. 20 crore. Licensed by Karnataka Food Safety Commissioner.
(d) Central license: Annual turnover above Rs. 20 crore or multi-state operations.

SECTION 6: KARNATAKA POLICE AND VERIFICATION
─────────────────────────────────────────

17. TENANT POLICE VERIFICATION IN KARNATAKA
(a) Landlords must conduct police verification of tenants.
(b) Online verification: Karnataka Police tenant verification portal.
(c) Documents required: Tenant's Aadhaar, PAN, photograph, current address proof, employer details.
(d) Failure to conduct police verification: Landlord may face legal liability.
(e) Passport verification: Separate process through police for passport applications.

18. KARNATAKA PREVENTION OF DANGEROUS ACTIVITIES ACT
Preventive detention act for persons engaged in dangerous activities — relevant for property disputes involving unlawful occupation.
