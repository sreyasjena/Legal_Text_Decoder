DELHI STATE SPECIFIC LAWS KNOWLEDGE BASE
========================================
State-specific legal reference for Delhi laws, property regulations, and compliance requirements.

─────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 1: STAMP DUTY AND REGISTRATION IN DELHI
─────────────────────────────────────────

1. STAMP DUTY IN DELHI
(a) Stamp duty rate: 6% for men, 4% for women of property value.
(b) Registration charges: 1% of property value.
(c) Stamp duty is calculated on the higher of: agreement value or circle rate/guidance value.
(d) Women buyers may get reduced stamp duty in Delhi — verify current rates.
(e) e-Stamp: Available for Delhi through SHCIL and authorized banks.
(f) Certificate number format: IN-DE-... for Delhi e-stamps.

2. PROPERTY REGISTRATION IN DELHI
(a) Registration portal: doris.delhigovt.nic.in
(b) All sale deeds for immovable property must be compulsorily registered.
(c) Documents required: Sale deed, ID proof of both parties, PAN cards, property documents, NOC if applicable.
(d) Biometric verification at Sub-Registrar office.
(e) Encumbrance Certificate: Available through registration portal — shows all registered transactions.

─────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 2: RENT CONTROL IN DELHI
─────────────────────────────────────────

3. RENT CONTROL ACT IN DELHI
Applicable law: Delhi Rent Control Act 1958 — applies to tenancies with monthly rent below Rs. 3,500
(a) Governs residential and commercial tenancies within the scope of the Act.
(b) Leave and license agreements properly executed are generally outside Rent Control Act protection.
(c) 11-month convention: Common practice to avoid Rent Control Act protection and mandatory registration.
(d) Disputes: Approach Rent Authority or Civil Court as applicable.

─────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 3: REAL ESTATE REGULATION IN DELHI
─────────────────────────────────────────

4. RERA IN DELHI
RERA portal: rera.delhi.gov.in
(a) All residential/commercial projects with area >500 sq. m. or 8+ units must be registered.
(b) Verify project registration before booking any under-construction property.
(c) File complaints online through the RERA portal for project delays or disputes.
(d) Homebuyer rights under RERA: Interest on delayed possession, refund with interest, compensation.

SECTION 4: STATE-SPECIFIC LAWS AND REGULATIONS
─────────────────────────────────────────

5. KEY STATE-SPECIFIC PROVISIONS IN DELHI
(a) DDA (Delhi Development Authority): Allots housing and develops land in Delhi.
(b) NDMC and MCD: Municipal bodies for property tax.
(c) Professional tax: Not applicable in Delhi.
(d) Delhi Shops and Establishments Act 1954.
(e) Circle rates: Published by Delhi government for stamp duty calculation.
(f) Agricultural land: Delhi Land Reforms Act 1954 governs agricultural land.

─────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 5: GENERAL LEGAL ADVICE FOR DELHI
─────────────────────────────────────────

6. PROPERTY TRANSACTION ADVICE FOR DELHI
(a) Always verify title documents in Delhi revenue record format.
(b) Check state-specific stamp duty before property registration.
(c) Verify RERA registration of under-construction projects on Delhi RERA portal.
(d) Consult a local advocate familiar with Delhi property laws for complex transactions.
(e) Check for agricultural land conversion requirements specific to Delhi.
(f) Verify Encumbrance Certificate from Delhi Sub-Registrar office.
(g) Conduct police verification of tenants as required by Delhi police department.
(h) Check society/association NOC requirements for apartment transactions in Delhi.
