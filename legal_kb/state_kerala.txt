KERALA STATE SPECIFIC LAWS KNOWLEDGE BASE
=========================================
State-specific legal reference for Kerala laws, property regulations, and compliance requirements.

─────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 1: STAMP DUTY AND REGISTRATION IN KERALA
─────────────────────────────────────────

1. STAMP DUTY IN KERALA
(a) Stamp duty rate: 8% (highest in India) of property value.
(b) Registration charges: 2% of property value.
(c) Stamp duty is calculated on the higher of: agreement value or circle rate/guidance value.
(d) Women buyers may get reduced stamp duty in Kerala — verify current rates.
(e) e-Stamp: Available for Kerala through SHCIL and authorized banks.
(f) Certificate number format: IN-KE-... for Kerala e-stamps.

2. PROPERTY REGISTRATION IN KERALA
(a) Registration portal: keralaregistration.gov.in
(b) All sale deeds for immovable property must be compulsorily registered.
(c) Documents required: Sale deed, ID proof of both parties, PAN cards, property documents, NOC if applicable.
(d) Biometric verification at Sub-Registrar office.
(e) Encumbrance Certificate: Available through registration portal — shows all registered transactions.

─────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 2: RENT CONTROL IN KERALA
─────────────────────────────────────────

3. RENT CONTROL ACT IN KERALA
Applicable law: Kerala Rent Control Act 1965 — strong tenant protection
(a) Governs residential and commercial tenancies within the scope of the Act.
(b) Leave and license agreements properly executed are generally outside Rent Control Act protection.
(c) 11-month convention: Common practice to avoid Rent Control Act protection and mandatory registration.
(d) Disputes: Approach Rent Authority or Civil Court as applicable.

─────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 3: REAL ESTATE REGULATION IN KERALA
─────────────────────────────────────────

4. RERA IN KERALA
RERA portal: k-rera.kerala.gov.in
(a) All residential/commercial projects with area >500 sq. m. or 8+ units must be registered.
(b) Verify project registration before booking any under-construction property.
(c) File complaints online through the RERA portal for project delays or disputes.
(d) Homebuyer rights under RERA: Interest on delayed possession, refund with interest, compensation.

SECTION 4: STATE-SPECIFIC LAWS AND REGULATIONS
─────────────────────────────────────────

5. KEY STATE-SPECIFIC PROVISIONS IN KERALA
(a) Kerala Land Reforms Act 1963: Ceiling on land holdings.
(b) CRZ: Strict coastal regulation zone regulations.
(c) Thandaper: Land ownership record in Kerala.
(d) Professional tax in Kerala: Up to Rs. 2,400 per year.
(e) Non-Kerala residents face restrictions on agricultural land purchase.

─────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 5: GENERAL LEGAL ADVICE FOR KERALA
─────────────────────────────────────────

6. PROPERTY TRANSACTION ADVICE FOR KERALA
(a) Always verify title documents in Kerala revenue record format.
(b) Check state-specific stamp duty before property registration.
(c) Verify RERA registration of under-construction projects on Kerala RERA portal.
(d) Consult a local advocate familiar with Kerala property laws for complex transactions.
(e) Check for agricultural land conversion requirements specific to Kerala.
(f) Verify Encumbrance Certificate from Kerala Sub-Registrar office.
(g) Conduct police verification of tenants as required by Kerala police department.
(h) Check society/association NOC requirements for apartment transactions in Kerala.
