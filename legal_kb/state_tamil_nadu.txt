TAMIL NADU STATE SPECIFIC LAWS KNOWLEDGE BASE
=============================================
State-specific legal reference for Tamil Nadu laws, property regulations, and compliance requirements.

─────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 1: STAMP DUTY AND REGISTRATION IN TAMIL NADU
─────────────────────────────────────────

1. STAMP DUTY IN TAMIL NADU
(a) Stamp duty rate: 7% of property value.
(b) Registration charges: 4% of property value.
(c) Stamp duty is calculated on the higher of: agreement value or circle rate/guidance value.
(d) Women buyers may get reduced stamp duty in Tamil Nadu — verify current rates.
(e) e-Stamp: Available for Tamil Nadu through SHCIL and authorized banks.
(f) Certificate number format: IN-TA-... for Tamil Nadu e-stamps.

2. PROPERTY REGISTRATION IN TAMIL NADU
(a) Registration portal: tnreginet.gov.in
(b) All sale deeds for immovable property must be compulsorily registered.
(c) Documents required: Sale deed, ID proof of both parties, PAN cards, property documents, NOC if applicable.
(d) Biometric verification at Sub-Registrar office.
(e) Encumbrance Certificate: Available through registration portal — shows all registered transactions.

─────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 2: RENT CONTROL IN TAMIL NADU
─────────────────────────────────────────

3. RENT CONTROL ACT IN TAMIL NADU
Applicable law: Tamil Nadu Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act 1960
(a) Governs residential and commercial tenancies within the scope of the Act.
(b) Leave and license agreements properly executed are generally outside Rent Control Act protection.
(c) 11-month convention: Common practice to avoid Rent Control Act protection and mandatory registration.
(d) Disputes: Approach Rent Authority or Civil Court as applicable.

─────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 3: REAL ESTATE REGULATION IN TAMIL NADU
─────────────────────────────────────────

4. RERA IN TAMIL NADU
RERA portal: tnrera.in
(a) All residential/commercial projects with area >500 sq. m. or 8+ units must be registered.
(b) Verify project registration before booking any under-construction property.
(c) File complaints online through the RERA portal for project delays or disputes.
(d) Homebuyer rights under RERA: Interest on delayed possession, refund with interest, compensation.

SECTION 4: STATE-SPECIFIC LAWS AND REGULATIONS
─────────────────────────────────────────

5. KEY STATE-SPECIFIC PROVISIONS IN TAMIL NADU
(a) Patta: Land ownership document (equivalent to Khata in Karnataka).
(b) Chitta: Village administrative officer's record of land ownership.
(c) Adangal: Village account record showing land cultivation details.
(d) CMDA (Chennai Metropolitan Development Authority): Development regulations.
(e) Professional tax in Tamil Nadu: Up to Rs. 2,400 per year.
(f) Tamil Nadu Shops and Establishments Act 1947.

─────────────────────────────────────────
SECTION 5: GENERAL LEGAL ADVICE FOR TAMIL NADU
─────────────────────────────────────────

6. PROPERTY TRANSACTION ADVICE FOR TAMIL NADU
(a) Always verify title documents in Tamil Nadu revenue record format.
(b) Check state-specific stamp duty before property registration.
(c) Verify RERA registration of under-construction projects on Tamil Nadu RERA portal.
(d) Consult a local advocate familiar with Tamil Nadu property laws for complex transactions.
(e) Check for agricultural land conversion requirements specific to Tamil Nadu.
(f) Verify Encumbrance Certificate from Tamil Nadu Sub-Registrar office.
(g) Conduct police verification of tenants as required by Tamil Nadu police department.
(h) Check society/association NOC requirements for apartment transactions in Tamil Nadu.
